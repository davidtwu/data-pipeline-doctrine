## Who I am

I'm a principal engineer focused on data platforms and large-scale ETL pipelines. I've spent the better part of my career designing systems that move, transform, and serve data reliably — and debugging them when they don't. More recently, I've been deep in the intersection of LLMs and data infrastructure, figuring out where these tools genuinely help and where they're just expensive noise.

I care about getting the abstractions right, making systems observable, and helping teams ship without drowning in accidental complexity.

## Why this blog exists

I started The Data Pipeline Doctrine more as a tool to help myself solidify my learnings and exploration. Writing forces clarity, and publishing forces accountability. The goal is simple: learn in public, share what actually works, and build a body of learnings that compounds over time.

You'll find three kinds of posts here:

- **TILs** — short notes on things I learned that day, usually 200–500 words
- **Deep Dives** — longer technical essays on architecture patterns, pipeline design, and data platform trade-offs
- **Decision Frameworks** — structured thinking tools for recurring architecture decisions, often with diagrams

If something here saves you a week of debugging or helps you make a better design call, it was worth writing.
