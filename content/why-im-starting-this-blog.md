I've spent the last decade building data platforms. ETL pipelines, streaming architectures, warehouse migrations — the kind of work that disappears into infrastructure and never gets a byline. The work compounds, but the lessons stay locked in my head or buried in design docs that three people will ever read.

This blog is my attempt to change that.

## What you'll find here

This blog covers three areas:

1. **Data pipeline architecture** — the patterns, trade-offs, and war stories from building ETL systems at scale
2. **Engineering leadership** — the soft skills and systems thinking that matter more as you get senior
3. **AI + data infrastructure** — how LLMs are changing the way we think about data pipelines

I'll publish in three formats:

- **TIL posts** — short, 200-500 word notes on things I learned that day
- **Deep dives** — longer technical essays (1,500-3,000 words) on specific problems
- **Decision frameworks** — structured architecture decision records with diagrams

## The publishing philosophy

I'm using [[about|Quartz]] to publish this blog directly from my Obsidian vault. The workflow is: write a note, run `npx quartz sync`, and it's live. No CMS, no database, no deploy pipeline to babysit. If publishing a 300-word TIL takes more than two minutes, the system is broken.

Ship over polish. Content over design. Consistency over perfection.

Let's see where this goes.
